Latest Mission: Perseverance Rover

Searching for Ancient Life

NASA's Perseverance rover landed in Jezero Crater on February 18, 2021. It collects rock cores and searches for signs of ancient microbial life. The rover also carries the Ingenuity helicopter, which performed the first powered flight on another planet.

Key instruments include SHERLOC (spectrometer) and MOXIE (experimental oxygen generator). The mission paves the way for human exploration of Mars.

Sample Return Campaign

Perseverance is caching rock samples that will be returned to Earth by a future joint NASA-ESA mission. Scientists hope these samples will answer whether life ever existed on the Red Planet.

Did You Know?

The Perseverance rover is the size of a small car – about 3 metres long, 2.7 metres wide, and 2.2 metres tall. It weighs roughly 1,025 kilograms (2,260 pounds).

Its top speed on Mars is just 0.1 miles per hour, but it has already travelled over 15 kilometres!
